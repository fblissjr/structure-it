## 1. PURPOSE

This **Anti-Corruption and Ethical Business Practices Policy** sets forth the mandatory standards of conduct required of all personnel of **[Company Name]** ("the Company") to ensure strict compliance with all applicable international, federal, and local anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws, including, but not limited to, the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the U.K. Bribery Act 2010, and the OECD Anti-Bribery Convention.

The purpose of this Policy is to mitigate the significant legal, financial, and reputational risks associated with corruption, bribery, fraud, and unethical business dealings. Adherence to these standards protects the integrity of the Company’s financial reporting, operational processes, and relationships with governments, customers, and third parties.

This Policy is directly aligned with **[Company Name]**’s core values of **Integrity, Transparency, and Accountability**, and supports our strategic objective of maintaining trusted partnerships across all global jurisdictions in which we operate.

## 2. SCOPE

### 2.1 Applicability

This Policy applies **universally** and **mandatorily** to:

1.  **All Employees:** Full-time, part-time, temporary staff, and interns of **[Company Name]** worldwide, regardless of seniority or functional department.
2.  **Directors and Officers:** Members of the Board of Directors and Executive Leadership Team.
3.  **Third Parties:** All agents, consultants, distributors, joint venture partners, suppliers, and any other representative acting on behalf of the Company, unless explicitly excluded via a formal, documented Third-Party Compliance Agreement (TPCA) approved by Legal.

### 2.2 Covered Systems and Processes

This Policy governs all activities related to:

*   Government interactions, including licensing, permitting, and customs clearance.
*   Sales, marketing, and procurement activities, particularly those involving state-owned enterprises (SOEs).
*   The maintenance and accuracy of the Company’s books and records, as required under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) Section 302 and 404.
*   The management and approval of Gifts, Hospitality, Travel, and Entertainment (GHTE).
*   Due diligence and onboarding of all Third-Party Intermediaries (TPIs).

### 2.3 Geographic Scope

This Policy is global in scope. All entities, subsidiaries, and operations of **[Company Name]** are subject to these requirements, irrespective of the local anti-corruption legislation, unless an explicit, documented exception is granted by the Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) and General Counsel (GC).

### 2.4 Exclusions

This Policy **does not** apply to:

*   Direct commercial transactions between **[Company Name]** and private commercial entities where no governmental nexus is present, provided that all transactions are recorded accurately in compliance with GAAP/IFRS.
*   Internal disciplinary actions governed solely by the Employee Handbook, provided such actions do not contradict the mandatory prohibitions herein.

## 3. DEFINITIONS

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bribery** | The offering, promising, giving, accepting, or soliciting of anything of value to improperly influence the actions of an official or other person in violation of a legal or official duty. |
| **Facilitating Payment** | A payment made to a government official to secure or expedite the performance of a routine governmental action to which the payer is otherwise entitled. (Note: Such payments are **prohibited** under this Policy, regardless of local legality.) |
| **Government Official (GO)** | Any employee or representative of a government entity, state-owned enterprise (SOE), political party, public international organization (e.g., UN, World Bank), or candidate for political office. This includes employees of SOEs providing commercial services. |
| **GHTE** | Gifts, Hospitality, Travel, and Entertainment provided to or received from any individual, specifically including Government Officials. |
| **Materiality Threshold** | The cumulative monetary threshold for requiring enhanced due diligence or pre-approval for GHTE, set at **\$500 USD** per event or **\$1,500 USD** aggregate in any single calendar quarter. |
| **Third-Party Intermediary (TPI)** | Any individual or entity (e.g., agent, consultant, customs broker) retained by the Company to interact with GOs or conduct business on the Company's behalf. |
| **Anything of Value** | Broadly defined to include money, cash equivalents, loans, gifts, services, employment opportunities, charitable donations, travel, lodging, entertainment, or any item intended to create an improper business advantage. |

## 4. POLICY STATEMENTS

### 4.1 Prohibition on Bribery and Corruption

1.  **MANDATORY:** Personnel **shall not**, directly or indirectly, offer, promise, authorize, or give **Anything of Value** to any **Government Official** for the purpose of influencing any act or decision, or to secure any improper advantage, in violation of the FCPA or U.K. Bribery Act.
2.  **MANDATORY:** Personnel **must not** solicit or accept **Anything of Value** from any person or entity if they know or suspect the item is intended to improperly influence **[Company Name]**’s business decision or grant an improper advantage to the provider.
3.  **PROHIBITED:** **Facilitating Payments** are **strictly forbidden** under this Policy. If an employee believes their safety or the immediate continuation of critical Company operations is at risk, they **must** comply with local demands but **shall** report the incident to the Compliance Department within **24 hours** of resolution.
4.  **MANDATORY:** Any employee involved in initiating, approving, or making a payment that they suspect constitutes bribery **must** escalate the transaction via the **COMP-002 Escalation Form** to the CCO and Legal Department immediately, and **shall not** proceed with the disbursement.

### 4.2 Accurate Books and Records (SOX Compliance)

5.  **MANDATORY:** All transactions, expenditures, and assets of the Company **must** be accurately and fairly recorded in the books and records in reasonable detail, consistent with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP/IFRS).
7.  **MANDATORY:** The Finance and Accounting departments **are required to** maintain internal accounting controls sufficient to provide reasonable assurances that transactions are authorized, executed, and recorded properly, in adherence to **SOX Section 404** requirements.
8.  **PROHIBITED:** **Shall not** create, maintain, or authorize the creation of any off-book accounts, slush funds, or hidden reserves for any purpose whatsoever.
9.  **MANDATORY:** Entries reflecting false or misleading expenditures, or payments disguised as legitimate business expenses (e.g., "Consulting Fees," "Commissions"), **must** be immediately flagged by the relevant Manager for mandatory review by Internal Audit.

### 4.3 Gifts, Hospitality, Travel, and Entertainment (GHTE)

10. **MANDATORY:** All GHTE provided to **Government Officials** **must** be pre-approved by the relevant Department Head and the Compliance Officer, irrespective of the monetary value.
11. **MANDATORY:** When offering GHTE to private commercial clients, the aggregate value **shall not** exceed the **Materiality Threshold** (\$500 per event / \$1,500 quarterly) unless documented justification and pre-approval from the VP level or higher is obtained.
12. **RECOMMENDED:** Employees **should** prioritize offering low-value promotional items (e.g., branded merchandise valued under \$100) over cash equivalents or extravagant entertainment.
13. **PROHIBITED:** The offering or acceptance of GHTE **must not** be made with the express or implied understanding that it is intended to influence a business decision.
14. **MANDATORY:** Any employee **receiving** GHTE from a Third Party or potential partner valued over **\$250 USD** **must** report the receipt to the Compliance Officer via the GHTE Log within **five (5) business days**.

### 4.4 Third-Party Intermediary (TPI) Management

15. **MANDATORY:** Due diligence on all TPIs engaged to interact with GOs or operating in high-risk jurisdictions **must** be conducted prior to engagement, following the tiered risk methodology outlined in **Procedure 5.1**.
16. **MANDATORY:** Contracts with TPIs **shall** include mandatory anti-corruption clauses, granting **[Company Name]** the right to audit the TPI's relevant books and records upon reasonable suspicion of misconduct.
17. **MANDATORY:** Compensation structures for TPIs **must** be reasonable and commensurate with the actual services performed. Any commission or fee exceeding **15%** of the total contract value **requires** review and explicit written approval from the CFO and CCO.
18. **CONDITION:** If a TPI's due diligence score places them in the "High Risk" category (as defined in the Risk Matrix, Appendix A), **then** their engagement **must** be reviewed annually by the Compliance Committee, and they **must** undergo mandatory anti-corruption training every **twelve (12) months**.
19. **PROHIBITED:** **[Company Name]** **shall not** utilize TPIs to funnel payments or transactions intended to bypass internal controls or circumvent this Policy.

### 4.5 Regulatory Compliance and Oversight

20. **MANDATORY:** All relevant personnel (Sales, Finance, Legal, Executive Leadership) **are required to** complete the **Annual Anti-Corruption Refresher Training** module within **30 days** of its release each fiscal year.
21. **MANDATORY:** The Compliance Department **shall** conduct a comprehensive, risk-based corruption audit of the **Top 5** highest-risk geographic regions (as identified in the annual **Enterprise Risk Assessment**) on a **biennial** (every two years) schedule.
22. **MANDATORY:** The CCO **must** provide a formal compliance report to the **Board of Directors Audit Committee** on the status of anti-corruption controls, TPI compliance, and any significant violations **quarterly**.
23. **MANDATORY:** Any changes to applicable anti-corruption laws **must** be assessed for impact on **[Company Name]** operations by the Legal Department within **forty-five (45) days** of official publication, with remediation plans submitted to the CCO thereafter.
24. **RECOMMENDED:** Department heads **should** foster an environment where employees feel safe raising ethical concerns without fear of reprisal, reinforcing the **Whistleblower Protection Procedures (COMP-005)**.

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Third-Party Intermediary (TPI) Due Diligence Procedure

This procedure addresses requirements **4.15** and **4.16**.

1.1. **Initiation:** The Business Owner requests engagement of a TPI and completes the **TPI Intake Form (Appendix B)**, detailing the scope of work and justification for the engagement.

1.2. **Risk Scoring:** The Compliance Analyst scores the TPI based on jurisdictional risk (Country Risk Index), scope of work (interaction with GOs), and ownership structure (beneficial ownership transparency). This generates a risk score (Low, Medium, High).

1.3. **Screening:** All TPIs **must** be screened against global watchlists, sanctions lists (OFAC, EU), and internal exclusion lists.

1.4. **Tiered Requirements:**
    *   **Low/Medium Risk:** Standard documentation review and execution of the **Standard TPCA**.
    *   **High Risk (Requirement 4.18):** Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) investigation, requiring background checks on Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs), mandatory in-person interviews, and approval by the **CCO and CFO**.

1.5. **Approval Workflow:** Final engagement approval flows from the Business Owner $\rightarrow$ Legal Review $\rightarrow$ Compliance Review. **No contract shall be executed** until the Compliance sign-off is secured.

### 5.2 Reporting and Investigation of Potential Violations

This procedure addresses requirements **4.3** and **8.3**.

2.1. **Reporting Channels:** Personnel **must** report suspected violations through one of the following secure, confidential channels:
    *   Directly to their Manager or Department Head.
    *   Directly to the Compliance Officer or Legal Department.
    *   Via the anonymous, third-party managed **Ethics Hotline (24/7)**.

2.2. **Initial Triage:** Upon receiving a report, the CCO or designated delegate **shall** log the complaint within **one (1) business day** and perform an initial assessment to determine if the allegation warrants formal investigation (i.e., credibility, specificity, and materiality).

2.3. **Investigation Initiation:** If warranted, the CCO **must** notify the General Counsel (GC) and the Chair of the Audit Committee within **48 hours**. An investigation team, typically comprising Legal and Internal Audit, **will** be formally chartered.

2.4. **Preservation:** Legal Counsel **is required to** immediately issue a **Legal Hold Notice** for all potentially relevant documents and electronic communications upon investigation chartering.

### 5.3 GHTE Pre-Approval Process

This procedure addresses requirement **4.10**.

3.1. **Submission:** The employee requesting to provide GHTE **must** submit the **GHTE Pre-Approval Request Form** via the expense management system at least **ten (10) business days** in advance of the proposed event.

3.2. **Required Data:** The request **must** clearly state: the recipient’s name and title, the recipient’s governmental status (if applicable), the business purpose, the exact monetary cost, and documentation confirming the event is permissible under local law.

3.3. **Review Thresholds:**
    *   Value $<\$ 500$ (Commercial): Approval by Direct Manager.
    *   Value $\geq \$ 500$ or Recipient is a GO (Commercial or Government): Approval required by **Department VP AND Compliance Officer**.

3.4. **Documentation:** All approved GHTE requests **shall** be retained by the Finance department for a minimum of **seven (7) years** to facilitate SOX compliance testing.

## 7. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

### 7.1 Monitoring and Verification

Compliance with this Policy **will be** monitored through a risk-based approach:

*   **Automated Controls:** Financial systems **shall** automatically flag disbursements exceeding the **\$1,500** threshold for manual review by Internal Audit unless pre-approved via Procedure 5.3.
*   **Internal Audit:** The Internal Audit function **is required to** annually test the effectiveness of TPI onboarding controls and GHTE documentation for **at least 15%** of high-risk transactions identified during the year.
*   **Certification:** All employees **must** digitally attest to reading and understanding this Policy during onboarding and annually thereafter, as part of the **Annual Certification Requirement**.

### 7.2 Consequences of Violations

Violations of this Anti-Corruption and Ethical Business Practices Policy **will** result in severe disciplinary action, up to and including:

1.  **Immediate Suspension** pending investigation.
2.  **Termination of Employment** for cause, regardless of the severity of the legal penalties incurred by the Company.
3.  **Clawback** of compensation or bonuses related to the period during which the violation occurred.
4.  **Referral** of the matter to appropriate governmental or regulatory authorities for civil or criminal prosecution, where applicable.

### 7.3 Non-Retaliation

**[Company Name]** strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who, in good faith, reports a suspected violation of this Policy or participates in an investigation. Reports made in bad faith or that are knowingly false may result in disciplinary action against the reporting individual.

## 8. EXCEPTIONS

### 8.1 Exception Request Process

1.  Requests for exceptions to any mandatory provision of this Policy **must** be submitted in writing to the Compliance Officer using the **COMP-002 Exception Request Template**.
2.  The request **must** provide a detailed business justification explaining why adherence to the standard policy provision is commercially impracticable or poses an undue hardship, and must demonstrate that the proposed action minimizes corruption risk (e.g., equivalent alternate controls).

### 8.2 Approval Authority

1.  Exceptions concerning TPI compensation structures (4.17) or high-risk TPI engagements (4.18) **require** approval by both the **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** and the **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)**.
2.  All other exceptions **must** be approved solely by the **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)**.
3.  No exception approval **shall** be valid for a period exceeding **twelve (12) months** and must be formally reviewed and re-approved before expiration. Documentation of all granted exceptions **must** be maintained by Legal.
