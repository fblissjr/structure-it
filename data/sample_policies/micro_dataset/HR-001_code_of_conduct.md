## 1. PURPOSE

This Code of Conduct establishes the minimum standards of professional behavior and ethical decision-making expected of all employees, contractors, and officers of [Company Name]. Adherence to this Code is fundamental to maintaining a trustworthy, productive, and legally compliant work environment.

## 2. SCOPE

This Code of Conduct applies to all individuals performing work for or on behalf of [Company Name], regardless of location, employment status (full-time, part-time, temporary), or seniority. This includes all employees, officers, directors, temporary staff, and external contractors working with company resources or representing the company externally.

## 3. DEFINITIONS

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Company Assets** | All tangible and intangible property owned or controlled by [Company Name], including hardware, software, intellectual property, confidential data, and facilities. |
| **Confidential Information** | Non-public business data, trade secrets, customer data (subject to GDPR/CCPA compliance), financial projections, and proprietary source code. |
| **Conflict of Interest** | A situation in which an employee's private interests could improperly influence, or appear to influence, their professional judgment or actions on behalf of the Company. |
| **Proprietary Information** | Information relating to the Company's business operations that is not generally known to the public (often overlaps with Confidential Information). |
| **Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)** | Information concerning the Company that a reasonable investor would consider important in making an investment decision (relevant under SEC regulations if publicly traded). |
| **Whistleblower** | An individual who reports suspected violations of law, regulation, or Company policy. |

## 4. POLICY STATEMENTS

### 4.1 Integrity and Compliance with Law

**4.1.1 Legal Obligation:** All employees **shall** comply with all applicable local, state, federal, and international laws and regulations governing the Company's operations, including, but not limited to, anti-corruption (FCPA), labor laws, and data privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA).

**4.1.2 Reporting Misconduct:** Any employee who suspects or becomes aware of potential violations of law or this Code **must** report the concern immediately through the established procedures (Section 6). Failure to report known, serious violations is a violation of this Code.

### 4.2 Respect in the Workplace and Non-Discrimination

**4.2.1 Fair Treatment:** [Company Name] is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment, intimidation, and discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other legally protected status. All employees **shall** treat colleagues, customers, and partners with dignity and respect.

**4.2.2 Workplace Safety:** Employees **must** adhere to all established safety procedures. Weapons of any kind are **prohibited** on Company premises, except as expressly permitted by law and approved in advance by Security Management.

### 4.3 Use of Company Assets

**4.3.1 Asset Protection:** Employees **shall** use Company Assets only for legitimate business purposes. Minimal incidental personal use of electronic resources (email, internet) **should** be infrequent and should not interfere with job performance or violate other policies.

**4.3.2 Data Security:** Employees accessing or handling Confidential Information or Proprietary Information **shall** follow all established IT Security policies regarding access controls, data encryption, and password strength. Sharing passwords with anyone outside of authorized technical support **is prohibited**.

### 4.4 Conflicts of Interest

**4.4.1 Disclosure Requirement:** Any actual or potential Conflict of Interest **must** be fully disclosed in writing to the employee's Manager and the Human Resources Department *before* engaging in the activity or relationship.

**4.4.2 Outside Employment:** Employment or financial interest in a direct competitor, supplier, or customer **is prohibited** without prior written approval from the CHRO. This approval will generally be denied if the activity consumes more than 10 hours per week or impairs the employee’s primary duties.

### 4.5 Gifts, Entertainment, and Bribery

**4.5.1 Monetary Limits:** Employees **shall not** offer or accept any gifts, gratuities, entertainment, or favors that could create the appearance of improper influence or obligate the giver or receiver. Cash or cash equivalents of any amount **are prohibited**.

**4.5.2 Acceptable Value:** Gifts or entertainment offered by third parties **should not** exceed a fair market value of **$150 USD per occasion**. Any gift exceeding $150 USD **must** be reported immediately to the Finance Department for approval and potential donation or surrender to the Company.

### 4.6 Insider Trading (Applicable to all Employees with access to MNPI)

**4.6.1 Prohibition:** Employees **must not** buy or sell securities of [Company Name] (or any other company) while in possession of Material Non-Public Information (MNPI).

**4.6.2 Confidentiality:** Employees **shall not** recommend the purchase or sale of Company securities to any other person, nor directly or indirectly communicate MNPI to any person who might use it for securities trading.

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 Procedure for Reporting Concerns (Whistleblowing)

1. **Immediate Reporting:** If an employee observes or suspects a violation of this Code, they **must** immediately notify one of the following parties:
    a. Their direct Manager, OR
    b. The Human Resources Department, OR
    c. The dedicated Compliance Hotline (available 24/7).

2. **Confidentiality:** The reporting employee’s identity **will** be kept confidential to the extent possible, consistent with the need for a thorough investigation.

3. **Investigation:** The CHRO or Legal Counsel **shall** initiate an objective investigation within 5 business days of receiving the report.

4. **Documentation:** All investigation findings, evidence, and actions taken **must** be formally documented and retained by the Legal Department for a minimum of 7 years.

### 5.2 Procedure for Declaring a Conflict of Interest

1. **Review Trigger:** Prior to accepting any outside directorship, consultancy, or significant investment in a known vendor/competitor, the employee **must** complete the "Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form."

2. **Managerial Review:** The form **shall** be reviewed by the employee's immediate Manager to assess immediate job impact.

3. **Final Approval:** The Manager **must** forward the form to the CHRO. The CHRO, in consultation with Legal, **will** issue a final written determination within 15 business days, stipulating whether the activity is permissible, requires mitigation, or is prohibited.

## 6. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

| Role | Responsibility Under This Code |
| :--- | :--- |
| **All Employees** | Read, understand, acknowledge receipt, and adhere to all standards in this Code. Promptly report suspected violations. |
| **Managers/Supervisors** | Act as primary role models for ethical behavior. Ensure team members understand this Code and address minor issues promptly. Escalate serious concerns immediately. |
| **Human Resources (HR)** | Maintain policy records, manage the annual acknowledgment process, facilitate conflict of interest disclosures, and serve as a primary reporting channel. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advise on legal interpretations of the Code, oversee complex investigations, and ensure compliance with relevant statutory obligations (e.g., SEC filing requirements). |
| **CHRO** | Owns the Code of Conduct, authorizes exceptions (if necessary), and ensures comprehensive training is provided annually. |

---

## 7. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

**7.1 Verification:** Compliance with this Code **will** be monitored through mandatory annual employee training and acknowledgment, internal audit procedures (particularly regarding expense reports and vendor management), and adherence to established reporting protocols.

**7.2 Consequences of Non-Compliance:** Any employee found to have violated the Code of Conduct **will** be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment, and may face legal action where appropriate. Disciplinary action **will** be applied consistently, based on the severity and impact of the violation. Retaliation against any individual who makes a good-faith report under Section 5.1 **is prohibited** and will result in severe disciplinary action.
